Popular Indian Dishes

Biryani

Biryani is a fragrant rice dish made with basmati rice, spices, and marinated meat (usually chicken, mutton, or beef) or vegetables. The dish is known for its rich aroma and flavorful layers.

Biryani is believed to have originated in Persia and was brought to India by Mughal rulers. It has since evolved into various regional styles, including Hyderabadi, Lucknowi (Awadhi), and Kolkata biryani.

Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

Butter chicken is a creamy, rich dish made with marinated chicken that is cooked in a spiced tomato-based gravy, enriched with butter and cream. The dish is known for its velvety texture and delicious flavor.

This dish originated in Delhi, where it was created by the chefs of the Moti Mahal restaurant in the 1950s. It has since become a staple in Indian cuisine and is popular worldwide.

Frequently asked questions

How do I place an order for ingredients?

After selecting a recipe, you can order ingredients through integrated services like Blinkit or Zepto with a single click. The app will guide you to the checkout page.

How can I buy ready-made meals?

You can place orders for ready-made meals via integrated food delivery apps like Zomato and Swiggy. Simply select your preferred dish and checkout.

What nutritional information does the app provide?

For every recipe, the app provides a breakdown of calories, proteins, fats, carbohydrates, and other essential nutrients to help you track your diet.

Can I share my recipes with others?

Yes, the app allows you to share your favorite recipes with friends and family through social media or via a direct link.

What is the Cooking Assistant feature?

The Cooking Assistant provides real-time guidance as you cook. It can read recipe instructions, suggest ingredient alternatives, and even notify you when it's time to move to the next step.

Is there a way to track my cooking progress?

Yes, the app allows you to track your cooking progress by marking steps as complete and setting timers for different stages of the recipe.
